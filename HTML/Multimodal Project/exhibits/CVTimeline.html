Coronavirus Timeline
 
Dot Dot Dot

December 31, 2019

On December 31, 2019 Chinese officials in Wuhan identified multiple cases of pneumonia from an unknown cause.
The next week, the outbreak was identified as a new coronavirus. Symptoms include fever, dry cough, and shortness of breath. Infections include respiratory failure and pneumonia.
Just four days after the outbreak was identified, China reported its first known death due to the coronavirus.

Dot Dot Dot

January 20th, 2020

On January 20th, a World Health Organization situation report detailed the first confirmed cases in Thailand, Japan, and South Korea. The next day, the United States reported its first confirmed coronavirus case.
Two days later, China placed Wuhan under quarantine, all modes of transportation within and out of the city were canceled.
A week after the WHO report, the WHO declared the outbreak a global public health emergency due to over 9,000 cases being reported worldwide.
A day later, January 31st, the White House announted that entry into the United Sates was banned for most foreign nationals that had traveled to China within the last 14 days.

Dot Dot Dot

February 1st, 2020

On February 1st, Princess Cruises confirmed that a man who had sailed aboard the Diamond Princess from Yokohama, Japan to Hong Kong had tested positive for the virus.
The next day, the first coronavirus related death was reported outside of China. A 44-year-old Whuan resident died in the Philippines.
Two days later, the Diamond Princess cruise ship was quarantined with around 3,700 people onboard.
The next week, February 7th, Dr Li Wenliang, the Chinese doctor that issued a warning about the coronavirus outbreak before it was offically recognized, died in Wuhan.
A day after, the first U.S. citizen died from coronavirus in Wuhan and the death toll in mainland China rose to numbers that surpassed the number of fatalities from the SARS outbreak in 2003.

Dot Dot Dot

February 11th, 2020

On February 11th, the WHO announced that the disease caused by the new coronavirus will now be known as COVID-19.
Three days later, Egypt confirmed that its first COVID-19 case while the global death toll passed 1,500.
The same day, an 80-year-old Chinese tourist died in France. This marks the first COVID-19 death recorded outside of Asia.
A week after the first COVID-19 death outside of Asia, Iran reported 2 cases. Hours after this report, officals reported that both patients died.
A day after 2 Iran COVID-19 cases, South Korea reported its first COVID-19 death while the number of confirmed cases rose to 104. This death in South Korea was the 9th confirmed death from the virus outside of China.

Dot Dot Dot

February 24th, 2020

On February 24th, Italy became the worst-hit country in Europe as COVID-19 cases spiked.
The same day, the U.S. stock market plummeted due to COVID-19 fears and the Dow Jones Industrial Average experienced the worst day in two years.
Two days after the stock market plummeting, California announced the first case in the U.S. with an unknown source of exposure. In that same day, Brazil also confirmed its first COVID-19 case.
On February 28th, Iran reported 34 deaths out of 388 confirmed COVID-19 cases. Iran became the country with the highest number of deaths due to COVID-19 outisde of China.
After that, President Donald Trump announced additional travel restrictions to Iran and increased warnings about traveling to Italy and South Korea. In the same day, the first death COVID-19 related death is recorded in the U.S.
